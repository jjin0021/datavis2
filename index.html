Comparing Domestic and International Students
at Australian Public Universities

According to the QS World University Rankings, Australia is home to 6 of the top 50 universities in the world. With The University of Melbourne, The University of New South Wales, The University of Sydney, Australian National University, Monash University, The University of Queensland all making the top 50.

As such, Australia has attracted numerous International students throughout the years.

This dashboard compares the number of Domestic and International students studying at Australian Public Universities across 2017-2020. In particular, exploring the distribution of students across each Australian State with consideration towards their Level and Gender.

Whereby, Level in this scenario refers to whether the student is studying a Postgraduate or Undergraduate degree, and Gender is limited to Female and Males.

Number of Postgraduate and Undergraduate Students

Domestic

- The vast majority of students study an Undergraduate degree.
- The number of students has consistently increased over time.

International

- There is an approx. 50/50 split between Postgraduate and Undergraduate students.
- The number of students have decreased since 2019, likely due to Covid-19.
